About the Conference
A Critical Conversation at a Defining Moment

An estimated 4.4 million U.S.-citizen children live with at least one undocumented parent, and more than 10 million people reside in mixed-status families. This interdisciplinary conference brings together nationally recognized scholars, legal practitioners, journalists, and community advocates to examine the legal threats, lived experiences, and resistance strategies of mixed-status families — exploring law, narrative, and transnational approaches as tools for protection and change.

Convened by the Civil Rights, Migration and Workplace Law Initiative at ASU Law, this full-day event offers a rare opportunity to engage with leading voices at the intersection of immigration law, civil rights, and family life in America.

Distinguished speakers and moderators
Keynote Speaker
César Cuauhtémoc García Hernández
Gregory H. Williams Chair in Civil Rights & Civil Liberties · Ohio State University College of Law
Author of Migrating to Prison and Welcome the Wretched

Professor García Hernández holds the Gregory Williams Chair in Civil Rights and Civil Liberties at Ohio State University, where he is a professor of law and director of OSU's Latinx Studies Program. He writes and teaches about the intersection of criminal and immigration law and has published three books and over three dozen scholarly articles, earning him recognition as one of the most-cited immigration law scholars in the United States. His analyses of policies affecting migrants regularly appear in The New York Times, Los Angeles Times, The Guardian, Time, and many other venues.

Conference Speakers
Distinguished Panelists & Moderators
Read Full Speaker Bios →

César Cuauhtémoc García Hernández

Gregory H. Williams Chair in Civil Rights & Civil Liberties · Ohio State University College of Law

Professor García Hernández writes and teaches about the intersection of criminal and immigration law. He has published three books — Welcome the Wretched, Migrating to Prison, and Crimmigration Law — and is among the most-cited immigration law scholars in the United States.

Vice Dean Angela Banks

Charles J. Merriam Distinguished Professor of Law · ASU Law · Panel 1 Moderator

Angela M. Banks is a legal scholar specializing in membership and belonging in democratic societies. She is a member of the Council on Foreign Relations and a graduate of Harvard Law School, the University of Oxford, and Spelman College.

Rose Cuison-Villazor

Professor of Law & Chancellor's Social Justice Scholar · Rutgers Law School · Panel 1 (Virtual)

Professor Cuison-Villazor is a distinguished scholar of citizenship, immigration, and property law at Rutgers Law School.

Cassaundra Rodriguez

Associate Professor of Sociology · University of Nevada, Las Vegas · Panel 1 (Virtual)

Dr. Rodriguez specializes in Latinx sociology, immigration, and citizenship. Her book Contested Americans: Mixed-Status Families in Anti-Immigrant Times was a finalist for the SSSP C. Wright Mills Award.

Caitlin Millat

Associate Professor of Law · ASU Law · Panel 1

Professor Millat writes on the intersection of education, family, and the state. Her work has appeared in the UCLA Law Review, Georgetown Law Journal, and NYU Law Review. She previously served as a Climenko Fellow at Harvard Law School.

Dr. Laura Dicochea

Dean of Students Senior Coordinator; PhD in Transborder Studies · ASU · Panel 1

Dr. Dicochea's research examines the educational trajectories of transnational and transfronterizo students. She partners with the Secretaría de Educación y Cultura of Sonora to develop pedagogical frameworks supporting return migrant students.

Karina Ruiz

Senator, Mexican Legislature; Former Executive Director, Arizona Dream Act Coalition · Panel 2 (Virtual)

Senator Ruiz is a prominent advocate for immigrant rights and educational equity in Arizona and Mexico.

Rafael A. Martínez, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Southwest Borderlands · College of Integrative Sciences and Arts, ASU · Panel 2

Dr. Martínez's award-winning book Illegalized: Undocumented Youth Movements in the United States analyzes the rise of Undocumented Youth Social Movements. He is Co-Director of the newly launched Latinx Oral History Lab at ASU.

Golden McCarthy

Executive Director · Phoenix Legal Action Network (PLAN) · Panel 2

Golden spent 10 years at the Florence Immigrant & Refugee Rights Project, becoming their first Deputy Director. She later served as a Policy Advisor at the Office of Refugee Resettlement working on federal policies impacting unaccompanied children.

Elvia Diaz

Independent Journalist and host of Notas con Elvia Diaz · Panel 2

Elvia Diaz is an award-winning journalist and commentator covering immigration, politics, and community issues in Arizona and the borderlands.

Krsna Avila

Legal Director · Path2Papers, a Project of Cornell Law School · Panel 2

Krsna immigrated from Mexico as a child and brings personal and professional immigration experience. He previously served as an attorney at the Immigrant Legal Resource Center and as legal services manager at Immigrants Rising. He earned his J.D. from Cornell Law School.

Anita Huízar-Hernández

Associate Director, Hispanic Research Center · ASU · Panel 2 Moderator

Dr. Huízar-Hernández is a scholar and leader at ASU's Hispanic Research Center, contributing to research and programming at the intersection of Latinx communities, migration, and higher education.

Tran Dang

Founder and Director · Rhizome Center for Migrants, Guadalajara, Mexico · Panel 3

Tran Dang founded and directs the Rhizome Center for Migrants in Guadalajara, Mexico, providing transnational support and reintegration services for deported individuals and mixed-status families.

Nicole Elizabeth Ramos

Director, Border Rights Project · Al Otro Lado, Tijuana, Mexico · Panel 3

Nicole Ramos is an attorney, movement strategist, and recognized expert on U.S. border policy and the right to seek asylum. She co-founded Al Otro Lado, a binational nonprofit providing legal, humanitarian, and social services to thousands annually.

Dr. Emir Estrada

Associate Professor, School of Human Evolution and Social Change · ASU · Panel 3

Dr. Estrada is a sociologist whose research focuses on immigration, childhood, and community dynamics in mixed-status families and Latinx communities.

Honorable Jorge Mendoza Yescas

Consul General of Mexico in Phoenix · Panel 3

Consul General Mendoza Yescas has over 20 years of diplomatic service. Since 2019, he has led initiatives including the recognition of the Mexican Consular ID as official identification in Arizona and the creation of the Entrepreneurship Program for Mexican Women Abroad with ASU.

Irasema Coronado

Director and Professor, School of Transborder Studies · ASU · Panel 3 Moderator

Dr. Coronado's research centers on human rights, migration, and social justice along the U.S.–Mexico border. She is a Fulbright scholar and received the Association for Borderland Studies Lifetime Achievement Award in 2023.

Salvador (Sal) Macias

Immigration & Criminal Defense Attorney · Macias Law Firm · Panel 4

Sal Macias is a practicing immigration and criminal defense attorney who represents detained and deportable individuals in Arizona.

Katie Ruhl

Managing Attorney · Florence Immigrant & Refugee Rights Project · Panel 4

Katie Ruhl leads legal services at the Florence Project, which provides free legal and social services to adults and unaccompanied children detained in Arizona immigration detention.

Juan Cuéllar Torres

Director of Education & Advocacy · Kino Border Initiative, Nogales, AZ · Panel 4

Originally trained as a lawyer in Colombia, Juan leads binational strategies to advance migrant justice. His prior roles include leading nationwide refugee program operations with USCRI and representing Colombia at the United Nations.

Reyna Montoya

Founder & CEO · Aliento · Panel 4

Reyna Montoya is a DACAmented social entrepreneur and community organizer born in Tijuana. Under her leadership, Aliento has impacted over 70,000 individuals and educated nearly 100,000 voters, playing a key role in passing Prop 308 in Arizona.

Professor Evelyn Cruz

Director, Immigration Clinic · Sandra Day O'Connor College of Law, ASU · Panel 4 Moderator

Professor Cruz is a Clinical Professor of Law and Director of the Immigration Clinic at ASU Law, where she oversees direct representation and training in immigration legal practice.
